Global land use — 1800 to 2023

The shrinking ecosystem — worldwide

All 33 billion acres of ice-free land on Earth, from pre-industrial baseline to today. The story is the same as the US, at larger scale — and with a crucial difference: the active deforestation frontiers are in the tropics, driven largely by export demand from wealthy countries.

The ratio that matters: Livestock uses 77% of all agricultural land globally but produces only 18% of global calories. Beef and dairy occupy more land than all crops combined — yet feed far fewer people.
